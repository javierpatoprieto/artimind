## Day 1: direction before pixels

The fastest way to ruin an AI campaign is to start generating. I start with direction.

Before a single image exists, I define the world your campaign lives in. Palette, light, mood, references, the attitude of the model, the texture of the fabric, the grain of the film. I build a tight mood board that reads like one cohesive idea, not a folder of pretty pictures.

This is the part most people skip, and it is the part that separates a campaign from a pile of renders. When the direction is locked, every image afterward inherits the same DNA. That coherence is what makes a viewer believe everything came from a single, expensive shoot.

Day 1 is strategy. Everything else is execution against it.

## Locking model and look consistency

The hard problem in AI fashion is consistency. The same face, the same body, the same garment, across thirty frames and three formats. Get this wrong and your campaign looks like a stock catalog.

I solve it before scaling. I lock the model — the same person, recognizable from a magazine cover to a thumbnail. I lock the look — the exact garment, the exact styling, the exact accessories, holding their shape and detail from shot to shot.

This is craft, not luck. It is the difference between "AI images" and a campaign your brand can stand behind. Once the model and the look are stable, I can move fast without the work falling apart. Speed only matters when the quality survives it.

## From look to full campaign

With direction set and consistency locked, the campaign opens up quickly.

One look becomes a story. The hero image becomes a series: different poses, different angles, different distances, the model moving through the world we defined on day 1. I add the supporting shots a real campaign needs — detail crops, atmosphere frames, the in-between moments that make a set feel lived-in rather than staged.

Then comes finishing. I upscale and refine every selected image with Magnific, where I am an official partner. This is where skin keeps its texture, fabric keeps its weave, and the whole set gains the resolution and polish a premium brand demands. It is the step that lets these images hold up on a billboard, not just a phone.

Working this way, I deliver roughly 5x faster than a traditional pipeline — because there is no waiting on logistics, only iteration on craft.

## Every format your brand needs

A campaign is not one image. It is one idea, dressed for every channel.

From the same locked direction, I deliver the full set:

- Editorial: wide, cinematic frames for your site, lookbook and press.
- Vertical and social: 9:16 crops built for the format, not awkwardly cut from a horizontal — composed so the story still reads on a feed.
